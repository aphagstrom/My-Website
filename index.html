About
The Short Version

I've spent 7+ years working at the intersection of advanced engineering systems and high-quality technical communication. My path started in journalism — covering U.S. Army officer training, reporting on international trade for China Daily, and editing at the Financial Times — before a deep pivot into applied mathematics and aerospace research.

At Oak Ridge National Laboratory, I built computational simulation pipelines for molecular dynamics research. At Brigham Young University's MAGICC Lab, I worked on DoD-funded missile guidance and control systems with Georgia Tech. Along the way, I earned three graduate degrees in mathematics and journalism.

What connects all of it: I can read the math, understand the engineering, and write documentation that makes both accessible. That combination — domain fluency plus editorial craft — is what I bring to defense and aerospace technical writing.
